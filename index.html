About

I'm a PhD student at MIT in the AeroAstro department and CSAIL, where I work with Julie Shah in the Interactive Robotics Group. I also collaborate closely with Andreea Bobu in the CLEAR Lab. My research focuses on robot learning from human input, specifically developing algorithms for efficient behavior adaptation and transfer across tasks. I’m excited about building systems that real users can easily work with outside the lab.

My work has been supported by the Hugh Hampton Young Fellowship at MIT and the National Defense Science and Engineering Graduate (NDSEG) Fellowship. I earned my SB and SM in Aerospace Engineering also from MIT.

Learning Contextually-Adaptive Rewards via Calibrated Features

Alexandra Forsey-Smerek, Julie Shah, Andreea Bobu

IEEE/ACM International Conference on Human-Robot Interaction (HRI), 2026

[paper] []

We introduce a framework for learning contextually adaptive rewards by separating context-invariant task preferences from feature-specific contextual adaptations. We present calibrated features as intermediate representations that capture how features should adapt to context, and we propose a new paired-comparison input for training them. We show this approach achieves a 10× improvement in sample efficiency over multitask baselines and enables users to effectively teach their personal contextual preferences.

Masked IRL: LLM-Guided Reward Disambiguation from Demonstrations and Language

Minyoung Hwang, Alexandra Forsey-Smerek, Nathaniel Dennler, Andreea Bobu

International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA), 2026

[paper] []

We introduce Masked IRL, a method that uses language to disambiguate which reward a user is optimizing when providing demonstrations. Concretely, we leverage large language models (LLMs) to produce state-relevance masks from language instructions, and enforce invariance of the learned reward to irrelevant state components. We show this method outperforms standard language-conditioning approaches, improving reward accuracy by up to 15%.

Automation from the Worker's Perspective

Ben Armstrong, Valerie Chen, Alex Cuellar, Alexandra Forsey-Smerek, Julie Shah

arXiv, 2024

[paper] []

We report findings from a survey of more than 9,000 workers across nine countries on attitudes toward automation. We find that workers have a generally optimistic, nuanced perspective. For example, workers who solve complex problems, feel valued by their employers, and are motivated to advance in their careers are more likely to see new technologies as beneficial.

Centralized Multi-agent Synthesis with Spatial Constraints via Mixed-Integer Quadratic Programming

Alexandra Forsey-Smerek, Ho Chit Su, Kevin Leahy

NASA Formal Methods (NFM), 2023

[paper] []

We address the challenge of centralized multi-agent synthesis for tasks specified in Signal Temporal Logic (STL) that require adherence to spatial constraints and coordinated team behaviors. We present a mixed-integer quadratic programming (MIQP) approach and empirically compare it to a mixed-integer linear programming (MILP) formulation, showing that each is better suited for different types of spatial constraints.

Situational Cues for Continuous Trust Calibration in Automated Systems

Alexandra Forsey-Smerek, Katya Arquilla, Julie Shah

International Conference on Environmental Systems (ICES), 2022

[paper] []

We propose Situational Trust Cues (STCs) as visual signals to promote appropriate user trust calibration in automated systems, mitigating both undertrust and overtrust that can degrade performance. We additionally present an experimental design for investigating the application of STCs to a dynamic mission operations environment.

Assessment of Depth Data Acquisition Methods for Virtual Reality Mission Operations Support Tools

Alexandra Forsey-Smerek, Cody Paige, Ferrous Ward, Don Derek Haddad, Lindsay Sanneman, Jessica Todd, Jennifer Heldmann, Darlene Lim, Dava Newman

IEEE Aerospace Conference (AERO), 2022

[paper] []

We highlight the importance of high-resolution depth data for enhancing the scientific return of future planetary missions via virtual reality (VR) tools. We compare four depth data acquisition methods and assess their suitability for supporting mission operations in immersive environments.
